* problem w traditional games? 
	the amazing thing about rom hacks is that they really up the stakes and the complexity of the story compared to the main line games ... mainline is literally for grade schoolers: evil team, legendary pokemon, explore & talk to people ... but with rom hacks you can tell a more complicated story 
	there's something uniquely human about wanting to tell your story ... when I was wanting to tell my story, I was caught between two eras: there's the era just before me that wasn't as comfortable with the internet, etc., and then there's the era ahead of me, which feels like it's moving way too fast ... this game was a way that I was able to explore that paradox: every generation gets comfortable with the technology it's born into while feeling like the next wave is going to cause irreparable social harms ... it's not like you're getting something crazy complex in these romhacks but you do get something that has a plot targeted at someone older


* Why crystal? 
	Gen 2 has a special place in my heart ... I had actually made a difficulty hack ~ 3 yrs ago in crystal called Crib Crystal just to see what it was like ... 
	the story of crystal just sort of called to me ... crystal is about a region that is holding on to the past as time is passing it by ... thats sort of the way I feel ... 
	in crystal, you have some maps like Violet, Ecruteak, Mahogany that are undeniably historic ... but then you also have the modern Goldenrod and Olivine where the creature comforts are intruding ... this really reminds me of the Studio Ghibli movie "my neighbor totoro" ... crystal & johto in particular is the proper setting for this story 
	something I find with gen 2 is that the limits of the sprite give them a certain charm ... it's the difference between hearing live music and listening to a HiFi CD ... live music, you can literally see the musicians strumming, improvising, ... in gen 2 the sprites, you can literally see the pixels, you can see how the artists did it ... 
		in the htyphlosion pic, maybe you can pull it up, but there's major limitaitons ... you get four colors: black, white, and two more ... in the htyplosion pic, you see how the artist here blended the purple and tan to make the flame appear dynamic ... 
	if I was going to remake the game from scratch, I might use a more modern base that lets me get a few more species in, but I'd still use gen 2 ... you are really forced to go on a budget w/r/t features ... even text boxes: you get to show up to 36 characters at a time in most of them ... 
		in the "text" pic, you can see that there's only up to two lines, 18 characters each ... it feels like writing a haiku every time you want to make new dialogue ... but it forces you to write really, really tight. The first draft of every piece of dialogue was probably 3-4x longer than the version that got put into the game ...

* what inspired you to create the game? how has it evolved ? 
	I have had versions of this game on my phone's notes app for a very long time ... I first started working on it in earnest when my daughter was born about 3.5 years ago ... feeding her with the bottle takes about 30 m when they're little, so I was just writing down an overview of the plot and what the characters would be like with one hand on the bottle and another going letter by letter on my phone ... I was reading a lot of Ursula K Le Guin, author of the Disposessed and The Ones Who Walk Away from Omelas, and that was also right after I was reading George Orwell's Farewell to Catalonia ... these are accounts of utopian places ... at the time, I was thinking about what the world would be like for my kids: what type of world would I want for them ... what attributes would I want to pass down ... in the case of the Dispossessed, it follows this breakaway faction of anarchists who live on the moon, and they find that even in their perfect society, there are still problems to solve, social problems of hierarchy are nearly impossible to avoid ... in the case of Farewell to Catalonia, this is George Orwells account of fighting in the Spanish Civil War ... one of the things that really stands out to me on that is how much he talks about how easy things are with modern technology and not wanting to go back ... I believe it's about some mechanical digging tool that speeds up the way they dig the trenches ... but that carried into the themes as well: even though he longs to not have to rely on some new technology, it saves him from manual labor ... 
	the original version of the game in my head was way more complex and probably would've gotten me a strongly worded letter from game freak ... in the current version, there's a few allusions to these anarchist cells throughout johto that are committing sabotage on Silph sites, but mostly it's just these newsletters ... I was going to have the player actively involved in these, sort of like final fantasy 7 ... the radio tower plot was very different, the lighthouse tower plot was very different, i'll just say that ... ultimately it just felt too far out of place for a pokemon game ... 
	when you're writing you get all these ideas of what you can do with characters and places ... I had an idea for historic violet city and the making of the sprout tower ... but when I was editing the script it was just such a slog: you finish this big stadium event, you sneak through the radio tower in the dark, and you're waiting and waiting to get to the western capital ... it just ruined the pacing ... * biggest challenge with maintaining it ? 
	making puzzles that are the right challenge is a huge difficulty ... there's a section now that I think is about right, you have to go back and forth a couple times in the whirl islands to make progress ... in my original plan, you were going back and forth many times, raising and lowering water levels like the water temple from Ocarina of time ... then I realized that I couldn't even solve the puzzle without a cheat sheet! It was so complicated ... I love puzzles, the old Zelda GBC games, but pokemon players are playing first for pokemon and second for overworld puzzles ... I'm happy with he way it is now, but it was a lot of work to get the balance just right to feel rewarding for most players who don't have the guide in front of them ... I ended up putting the more complicated puzzles in optional areas, like the cianwood cave, the western glacier, and the kimono girls' trick house ... 


* go back and change one thing ? 
	I do wish that I'd found a way to get away from going through johto in the exact same way we always do ... when I made it the first time, I thought: i'm not going to go through johto starting with falkner's badge and ending with clair! ... but it still basically follows that path of going north first then west then east ... I probably would have tried to keep the player out of the western capital / ecruteak as long as possible, probably by smuggling them up to the sinjoh camp first ...
